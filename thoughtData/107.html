DD1

Function: Sinusoidal body movement. DD's receive input from VA and VB neurons, and hence, are likely to be active during ventral muscle contractions. They form neuromuscular junctions dorsally that coordinately relax the dorsal muscles during ventral muscle contraction (Driscoll and Kaplan, 1997). D-type neurons may also regulate the wave amplitude of the sinusoidal movement since unc-25 and unc-30 mutants that lack functional D neurons generate rhythmic sinusoidal movement with a reduced amplitude (McIntire et al., 1993).
