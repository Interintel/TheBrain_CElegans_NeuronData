PLMR

Neurotransmitter: Glutamate. Expresses VGluT, EAT-4 (Lee et al., 1999).
Function: Along with AVM and ALM, sense touch to the body and provide input to the command (inter)neurons (PVC, AVB, AVD, AVA) via both synaptic connections and gap junctions (Chalfie et al., 1985). The touch cells form gap junctions with agonist interneurons and chemical synapses with the antagonist interneurons. Hence, the anterior touch cells PLML/R form gap junctions with the forward movement interneuron PVC, but they provide synaptic input to the backward interneurons AVA and AVD (Kaplan and Driscoll, 1997; Goodman, 2006).
