ALA

Function: Involved in inducing normal lethargus quiescence (i.e., cessation of pharyngeal pumping and locomotion during the lethargus periods (an EGF/LET-23-induced sleep-like state prior to molts). ALA neuron inhibits locomotion by inhibiting AVE, which normally functions to promote locomotion. Synapse between ALA and AVE is contacted by the CEPsh glia and CEPsh cells inhibit synaptic transmission from ALA to AVE, promoting locomotion.
(Mulcahy and Ient 2010; Van Buskirk and Sternberg, 2007)
